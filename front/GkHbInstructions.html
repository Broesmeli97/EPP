Instructions for Registering Fonts


These instructions are applicable for Windows computers only. If you have any difficulties following them, please feel free to email us.

Remember that you can use this product without registering these fonts. Registration of these fonts is only required if you want to read the original Greek and Hebrew text.


Method One —

  1. Click on the Start Menu.

  2. Click on Settings. (Windows XP users can click just Control Panel and skip the next step.)

  3. Click on the Control Panel.

  4. Double-click on the Fonts folder.

  5. Click on the File menu.

  6. Click on the Install New Font option. A dialog box will appear in the center of your screen.

  7. There is a drop-down list titled "Drives" in the right corner of the dialog box. Select the CD-ROM drive that contains the EPP CD from this list.

  8. There is a list entitled "Folders" in the lower left corner of the dialog box. Double-click the "fonts" folder.

  9. There is an area entitled "List of Fonts" at the top of the dialog box. This should now be displaying two names, SPIonic (TrueType) and SPTiberian (TrueType).

  10. Select both of these fonts by clicking one, holding down the "shift" key and clicking the second one. Release the "shift" key.

  11. Ensure that the "Copy fonts to Fonts Folder" option (in the lower right corner of the dialog box) is checked.

  12. Press OK.


Method Two —

  1. Open up either My Computer or Windows Explorer.

  2. Right click on to the CD-ROM drive that is holding the EPP CD.

  3. Click "Open."

  4. Double click on the "fonts" folder.

  5. Select the two TTF files (SPTIBERI.TTF and SPIONIC_.TTF) by clicking and dragging.

  6. Click on the Edit menu, and then click Copy.

  7. Navigate to the hard drive that has the Windows operating system installed on it.

  8. Double click on the WINDOWS folder.

  9. Double click on the Fonts folder.

  10. Click on the Edit menu and click Paste.

  11. The two font files will be automatically installed.
