Forty Scripture Directions

 


1.      Never let the world come into God's room. (1 John 2:15)

2.      Keep up the same opinion now of things of the world, that you will have when you come to die. (Matt 16:26)

3.      Be not strangers to yourselves, but often commune with your own hearts. (Ps 4:4)

4.      Begin early in the service of God, and give Him the days of your youth. (Eccles 12:1)

5.      Shun the company that shuns God, and keep the company that God keeps. (Ps 119:63)

6.      Put a high value upon precious time, and lose none of it by sloth. (Eph 5:16)

7.      Allow a little time every day to think upon eternity. (Matt 25:46)

8.      Throughout your whole life be mindful of the chief business of life. (Phil 2:12)

9.      Let the rational soul govern, and the body be subservient to it. (1 Cor 9:27)

10.  Study both to live and die in the exercise of repentance and godly mourning for sin. (Matt 5:4)

11.  While you mind the necessary study of holiness, or inherent righteousness, let it never take the place of imputed righteousness. (Rom 10:3)

12.  Employ Christ as your surety in all cases, and in every strait go to Him for relief. (Ps 119:122)

13.  As you expect to live with Christ in heaven, study to live to Christ on earth. (2 Cor 5:15)

14.  Cleave fast to Christ, and the way of holiness, whatever it may cost you. (Acts 11:23; 2 Tim 3:12)

15.  Seek the approval of God, above that of men. (2 Cor 10:18)

16.  Be much employed in admiring God's redeeming love, and Christ's suretyship for us. (1 John 3:1,16).

17.  Delight much in thinking and speaking of Christ, and particularly of His death and sufferings for us. (Luke 9:31)

18.  Treat objections raised against your closing with Christ, as suggestions of the enemy, that is seeking the ruin of your souls. (Matt 16:23; Lam 3:25)

19.  Study much these great Gospel lessons of denying yourselves, and exalting God's free grace. (Matt 16:24; 1 Cor 1:31)

20.  Draw all your strength for work and warfare from Christ and present all your duties to God in Christ's name. (Isa 45:24; Col 3:17)

21.  In all the actions of your life, set the Lord still before you. (Ps 16:8; 25:15)

22.  Whether ye eat or drink, or whatsoever ye do, do all to the glory of God. (1 Cor 10:31)

23.  Spend as much time as possible in secret prayer and converse with God. (1 Thess 5:17)

24.  Make conscience of family religion, as well as of closet duties. (Josh 24:15; 2 Sam 6:20)

25.  Begin and end every day with God. (Ps 4:8; Ps 5:3)

26.  Seeing none knoweth what a day may bring forth, spend every day as if it were to be your last. (Prov 27:1)

27.  Sojourn in this world as travellers, keeping so loose from the world as to be able to pack up and be gone from it upon short warning. (1 Pet 1:17; 1 Cor 7:29-31)

28.  Keep up a lively sense of sacramental vows and engagements and walk answerably thereto. (Ps 56:12-13)

29.  Improve the talents God gives you for His service and glory. (Luke 19:13)

30.  Let all believers be thankful to God for His distinguishing favours and mercies to them. (Ps 116:8,12)

31.  Since God distinguishes you from others, in His mercies to you—see that you distinguish yourselves from others, in your service to Him. (Matt 5:47)

32.  Be not content with a fair profession of religion, or with a form of godliness, and a name to live among men. (Titus 1:16)

33.  Keep always a watch upon the door of your lips. (Ps 141:3)

34.  Walk cheerfully and contentedly in every condition. (Phil 4:11)

35.  Keep always the first and second table of the law joined together and make conscience of both. (Matt 22:37)

36.  Carefully guard against apostasy and backsliding from God and His ways. (Heb 10:38-39)

37.  Be watchful against all sin, and particularly your darling or constitutional sin, the sin that most easily besets you. (Ps 18:23; Heb 12:1)

38.  Be heartily concerned for the coming and enlarging of Christ's kingdom in the world. (Matt 6:10; Ps 72:19)

39.  Be busy laying up a stock for a sickbed or deathbed, when all the comforts of life shall be tasteless to you. (1 Tim 6:19)

40.  Be looking and longing for the eternal supper of the Lord. (Phil 1:23; Rev 19:9)
